---
title: "2018 Camp Fire — Deadliest California Wildfire on Record"
location: "Paradise, Butte County, California, USA"
year: 2018
type: incident
source: "California Department of Forestry and Fire Protection (CAL FIRE) Camp Fire Incident Report; USGS; NASA FIRMS; Congressional Research Service; Paradise town official records"
---

The Camp Fire ignited on 8 November 2018 near Camp Creek Road in Butte County, California, driven by strong Diablo winds and critically low humidity following an exceptionally dry autumn. It spread rapidly downslope into the town of Paradise.

The fire destroyed approximately 18,804 structures and killed 85 people, making it the deadliest and most destructive wildfire in California history. The entire town of Paradise (population ~27,000) was effectively destroyed within hours of ignition. The total burned area was approximately 62,053 ha (153,336 acres).

Fire spread rates in Paradise exceeded the ability of residents to evacuate via limited road networks, creating mass traffic gridlock. Many survivors fled on foot or sheltered in place. Victims were found in their vehicles, homes, and yards.

CAL FIRE determined the cause was electrical transmission lines owned by Pacific Gas and Electric Company (PG&E). PG&E subsequently pleaded guilty to 84 counts of involuntary manslaughter and filed for bankruptcy, reaching settlements totaling over $13.5 billion with survivors and affected local governments.

The Camp Fire highlighted the intersection of wildland-urban interface (WUI) expansion, utility infrastructure aging, extreme wind events, and prolonged drought as compounding risk factors. Post-fire satellite imagery and NASA FIRMS data showed the complete consumption of vegetated parcels surrounding Paradise, with dNBR maps confirming high-severity burn across the WUI zone.

The disaster accelerated California's vegetation management programs, utility infrastructure hardening requirements, and Public Safety Power Shutoff (PSPS) protocols.
