---
title: "2023 Evros (Dadia) Wildfire — Largest Wildfire Ever Recorded in the EU"
location: "Evros regional unit, northeastern Greece"
year: 2023
type: incident
source: "Copernicus EMS activation EMSR681; EU Joint Research Centre EFFIS; Greek Civil Protection press releases; WWF Greece post-fire reports"
---

The Dadia wildfire ignited on 19 August 2023 in the Evros regional unit of northeastern Greece, inside and adjacent to Dadia National Park (Natura 2000 site GR1110006). Driven by strong northerly Etesian winds and a preceding heat wave, the fire expanded rapidly and burned for approximately 16 days before being brought under control.

EFFIS (European Forest Fire Information System) estimated the total burned area at approximately 96,000 ha, making it the largest wildfire ever recorded in the European Union in the modern satellite monitoring era. The fire destroyed large portions of the park's old-growth oak-pine forests, which host one of Europe's last breeding colonies of the globally endangered Black Vulture (Aegypius monachus).

The Greek government declared a state of emergency in Evros. Several villages were evacuated and at least two fatalities were attributed to the fire. EU Civil Protection Mechanism assets — Canadair CL-415 aircraft from France, Italy, and Croatia — were deployed under the rescEU aerial firefighting reserve.

Copernicus EMS activation EMSR681 produced rapid mapping products using Sentinel-2 and Pleiades imagery. Post-fire dNBR analysis confirmed high-severity burn scars across much of the park's core zone. The fire occurred alongside several other major simultaneous fires across Greece (Rhodes, Alexandroupolis area), making the 2023 season the most destructive in Greek recorded history.
