---
title: "2018 Mati (Attica) Wildfire — Deadliest European Wildfire in Modern History"
location: "Mati, East Attica, Greece"
year: 2018
type: incident
source: "Greek Coroner's Office; Copernicus EMS activation EMSR280; EU Joint Research Centre; Human Rights Watch report 2019; Greek Parliament inquiry committee"
---

The Mati wildfire ignited on 23 July 2018 in the coastal settlement of Mati, approximately 30 km east of Athens in the municipality of Marathonas, East Attica. The fire spread within minutes through densely built residential areas directly to the sea, trapping residents and vacationers.

The official death toll reached 102 confirmed fatalities, making it the deadliest wildfire in Europe in the modern era. Dozens of victims were found in groups near the shoreline, having been overtaken while attempting to reach the sea. Hundreds were injured and over a thousand structures were destroyed.

The fire burned approximately 1,000 ha of residential, forested, and scrubland area over a very short period of intense activity. Extreme fire behavior was attributed to a combination of strong, shifting Etesian winds, severe drought, and the dense unplanned construction that had developed over decades without firebreaks or cleared evacuation corridors.

Copernicus EMS activation EMSR280 provided rapid mapping products that documented the burn extent and structural damage.

Post-incident investigations revealed systemic failures: illegal construction in forested areas, absence of a village street plan making evacuation routes impassable, delayed emergency response, and breakdown of fire suppression coordination. Criminal prosecutions followed against municipal officials and firefighting commanders.

The Mati disaster prompted major reviews of Greek civil protection law, illegal construction enforcement, and urban-wildland interface planning regulations.
