---
title: "2007 Peloponnese Wildfires — Deadliest Greek Fire Season Pre-2018"
location: "Peloponnese peninsula, southern Greece"
year: 2007
type: incident
source: "Greek Ministry of Environment; EFFIS; European Commission report on the 2007 Greek fires; WWF Greece; Reuters/AP contemporary coverage"
---

The summer of 2007 produced the most destructive fire season in Greece since systematic records began, centered primarily on the Peloponnese peninsula. A series of simultaneous fires ignited across Achaia, Ilia, Arcadia, and Messenia prefectures in late August 2007.

The largest single fire burned in the Ilia prefecture, destroying ancient olive groves, pine forests, and the landscape surrounding the ancient sanctuary of Olympia. The total burned area across the Peloponnese exceeded 170,000 ha, representing the vast majority of the national total of approximately 270,000 ha burned across Greece in 2007. These figures made 2007 the worst fire year in Greek recorded history until the 2023 season.

At least 65 people died in Peloponnese fires, primarily in the villages of Artemida, Grammatiko, Dafni, and Makistos in Ilia prefecture, often trapped in homes or while attempting to flee on rural roads. Total Greek fire fatalities in 2007 reached approximately 77.

The fires generated significant political controversy. Accusations of arson for illegal land development (land grabbers exploiting laws restricting construction on forested land), combined with government delays in deploying EU assistance, led to criminal investigations and the resignation of several officials.

EU Civil Protection Mechanism aerial assets were deployed. The 2007 fires led to lasting reforms in Greek civil protection law, including tougher anti-arson provisions and an extended 10-year ban on development of burned land.
