---
title: "2023 Rhodes Wildfire"
location: "Rhodes island, South Aegean, Greece"
year: 2023
type: incident
source: "EU Copernicus EMS activation EMSR683; Greek Civil Protection; EFFIS; BBC and Reuters news coverage July 2023"
---

The Rhodes wildfire ignited on 18 July 2023 near Kiotari on the southeastern coast of Rhodes island and spread rapidly northward driven by strong winds and drought conditions. The fire burned for approximately ten days before being contained.

EFFIS estimated the burned area at roughly 12,500 ha, predominantly consisting of pine forest, maquis scrubland, and rural land. The fire was among the largest ever recorded on the island.

The human impact was significant: Greek Civil Protection authorities ordered the evacuation of at least 19,000 residents and tourists from coastal resorts and villages including Kiotari, Gennadi, Pefki, and Lindos-area communities. It was described at the time as the largest evacuation in Greek history, with emergency ferries used to move evacuees from beaches.

EU Civil Protection Mechanism assets were deployed via the rescEU aerial firefighting reserve, with Canadair CL-415 aircraft from Croatia, France, Spain, and Italy assisting Greek Air Force aircraft. Ground firefighting teams from several EU member states were also dispatched.

Copernicus EMS activation EMSR683 produced rapid mapping products and burn severity analysis using Sentinel-2 imagery. Post-fire dNBR maps confirmed high-severity burn scars across interior forested zones.

The fire occurred during a prolonged heat wave affecting most of Greece, with temperatures exceeding 40°C in several regions. Rhodes 2023 was part of a historically destructive fire season that also included the Evros/Dadia fire (96,000 ha) and fires near Alexandroupolis, making 2023 the most damaging season in Greek recorded history.
