The European Forest Fire Information System (EFFIS) is operated by the Joint Research Centre (JRC) of the European Commission. It provides harmonized near-real-time and historical wildfire monitoring data for EU member states and participating countries in Europe, the Middle East, and North Africa.

**Fire danger forecasting**: EFFIS produces daily fire danger forecasts using the Canadian Fire Weather Index (FWI) system, computed from ECMWF ERA5 and operational meteorological data. Daily maps at ~10 km resolution are published publicly and used by national civil protection agencies.

**Burned area monitoring**: EFFIS uses MODIS (250 m, near-real-time) and Sentinel-2 (10–20 m, post-event) for burned area delineation. The EFFIS Fire Database records burned area statistics from 2000 onward for all EU and candidate countries.

**Key statistics (2000–2023)**:
- Annual average burned area in the EU: approximately 400,000–500,000 ha (excluding outlier years)
- Countries with highest recurring burned area: Portugal, Spain, Greece, Italy
- Largest single-country seasons: Portugal 2017 (~540,000 ha), Greece 2007 (~270,000 ha), Greece 2021 (~105,000 ha), Greece 2023 (~200,000+ ha total)
- The 2023 Dadia/Evros fire (~96,000 ha) was the largest individual fire ever recorded in the EU in the Copernicus/MODIS satellite monitoring era

**Fire Weather Index thresholds used by EFFIS**: Low <5.2, Moderate 5.2–11.2, High 11.2–21.3, Very High 21.3–38.0, Extreme >38.0. These differ from the unitless hot_dry_windy composite index used in operational agent outputs.

**Data access**: Historical EFFIS data, including burned area perimeters, are freely downloadable via the EFFIS Data Server. Current-season near-real-time detections are available via GWIS (Global Wildfire Information System).
