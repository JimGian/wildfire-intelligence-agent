The EU Civil Protection Mechanism (EUCPM), established under Decision No 1313/2013/EU and significantly strengthened by Regulation 2021/836, coordinates disaster response assistance among EU member states and participating states when national capacities are overwhelmed.

**Activation process**: When a disaster exceeds national response capacity, the affected country submits a request through the Emergency Response Coordination Centre (ERCC) in Brussels. The ERCC matches requests against registered national assets in the European Civil Protection Pool (ECPP) and alerts member states. Deploying states bear transport costs; the EU co-finances up to 75% of eligible costs for pre-committed pool assets.

**rescEU aerial firefighting fleet**: Following the 2017 and 2018 European fire seasons, the EU created the rescEU reserve — a strategic stock of assets owned or leased at EU level, managed by the Commission, and deployed on Commission decision rather than voluntary member state offer. The aerial firefighting component includes Canadair CL-415 amphibious scooper aircraft and helicopters. As of 2023, rescEU maintained aircraft stationed in France, Italy, Spain, Croatia, and Sweden.

**2023 deployment**: During the 2023 Greek fire season (Rhodes, Evros, Alexandroupolis), ERCC coordinated the largest peacetime civil protection operation in EU history at that point. Aircraft from France, Italy, Croatia, and Spain were deployed under both ECPP and rescEU frameworks. Ground firefighting teams from 10+ member states assisted.

**ERCC monitoring**: The ERCC operates 24/7 and uses the Copernicus Emergency Management Service (EMS) to provide near-real-time satellite mapping to support operational coordination during active deployments.
