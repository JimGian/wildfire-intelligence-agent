location: "Pedrógão Grande, Leiria district, Portugal"
year: 2017

source: "Portuguese Technical Commission of Independent Evaluation (CTAI) report 2017; EU Joint Research Centre; EFFIS; Reuters; Comissão Técnica Independente report to Portuguese parliament"
---

The Pedrógão Grande wildfire ignited on 17 June 2017 in the municipality of Pedrógão Grande in central Portugal, approximately 200 km northeast of Lisbon. The fire spread rapidly through eucalyptus and pine plantations under extreme heat, low humidity, and strong winds associated with a dry thunderstorm.

The fire killed 66 people on 17–18 June alone, predominantly on the N236 road between Figueiró dos Vinhos and Castanheira de Pêra where motorists were overtaken by fire while attempting to evacuate. A subsequent related fire complex in October 2017 (Oliveira do Hospital) killed an additional 45 people, bringing the combined 2017 toll to over 110 deaths — the worst wildfire disaster in Portuguese history.

The June fire burned approximately 44,500 ha. A subsequent independent technical evaluation (CTAI) criticized the fire suppression response, the flammability of eucalyptus and pine monocultures dominating the Portuguese forest landscape, inadequate road networks in rural areas, and failures in the warning systems and civil protection coordination.

The disaster prompted major policy debates in Portugal about eucalyptus plantation management, mixed-species afforestation requirements, and rural land abandonment (with consequent fuel accumulation). It also influenced EU-level discussions about forest management and wildfire risk under climate change.

The Pedrógão fire is frequently cited as a reference event in European wildfire risk assessments for its demonstration of how fire behavior in eucalyptus/pine stands can produce rapid rates of spread with lethal consequences.
