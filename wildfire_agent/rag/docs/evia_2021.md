---
title: "2021 Evia Wildfire"
location: "Evia island (northern), Central Greece"
year: 2021
type: incident
source: "Copernicus EMS activation EMSR517; EFFIS; EU Joint Research Centre; Greek Civil Protection; WWF Greece post-fire assessment 2021"
---

The northern Evia wildfire ignited on 3 August 2021 near Rovies and burned for approximately twelve days, becoming one of the most destructive wildfires in modern Greek history prior to the 2023 season.

EFFIS estimated the total burned area at approximately 50,500 ha, making it the largest single fire in the 2021 Greek fire season. The fire destroyed vast areas of fir forest (Abies cephalonica) and pine woodland on the slopes of northern Evia, habitats that take decades to regenerate.

Around 2,600 residents were evacuated from villages including Gouvès, Avgaria, Dafni, and Pefki. The fire destroyed approximately 150 homes and several agricultural buildings. One shepherd was killed.

The EU Civil Protection Mechanism was activated, with aerial firefighting assets deployed from multiple member states. Ground teams from France, Romania, and Ukraine assisted Greek firefighters.

Copernicus EMS activation EMSR517 delivered post-fire damage maps showing that over 50% of northern Evia's forested area was destroyed or severely damaged. The fire also burned within the Natura 2000 network site GR2420001, threatening protected fir forest ecosystems.

Post-fire assessments by WWF Greece documented severe erosion risk and loss of carbon stocks. The 2021 Evia fire became a symbol of Greece's vulnerability to increasingly severe fire seasons linked to climate change, prompting parliamentary debate and civil protection policy reviews.
